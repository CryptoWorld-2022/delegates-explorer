FAQ

Staking Characteristics
  1. Only the top 50 delegates are elected as block verifiers and will receive (and share) a block rewards.
  2. The block reward is given to the block verifier responsible of forging the block, and shared with their voters.
  3. Reserve proof-based voting/staking system: the XCASH’s stake always stays in your wallet.
  4. There is no lockup period. The staked XCASH always remain in your wallet and you can use them at any time. However, moving them from your wallet will cancel your entire staking<
  5. No need to keep your wallet/computer online if you are staking towards a shared delegate.
Voting Rules
  1. The minimum stake to vote for a delegate is 2 million XCASH.
  2. The election process occurs in every block.
  3. One vote per wallet

    Only one reserve proof will be valid per wallet address. This means you can only vote for one delegate per wallet address. If you want to vote for another delegate, you will need to create a new wallet, and send to it XCASH you plan to vote with.

  4. New votes will be taken into account at the top of the next hour.

    If you apply a new vote at XX:30, it will be in effect at XX+1:00.

  5. Your current vote will automatically get cancelled if you:

    - vote for another delegate
    - spend (send a tx) from your wallet (It is recommended to stake from a wallet you are not actively using.)

  6. There are no voting fees. You can cast a new vote or switch your vote as many time as you like.
 Solo Delegates, Private Group Delegates, Shared Delegates and Seed nodes

You will find 4 types of delegates:

  1. Shared Delegates groups

    The shared delegates are public delegates for whom you can vote. They will redistribute your share of the rewards minus their fees. As a voter, you should favor shared delegates.

  2. Solo Delegates person_outline

    Solo delegates are elected by themselves and are keeping the reward for them. You can vote for solo delegates, but don't expect to receive a share of the block reward.

  3. Private groups lock

    Private delegates group are delegates that will only redistribute shares of the block reward to a select group of their voters. They are usually decided with the delegate in private, so you shouldn't vote for them if you haven't communicated with the delegate first.

  4. Seed nodes admin_panel_settings

    Seed nodes help verify the block, keep the database. They are a trusted source to get the top 50 delegates from the daemon and keep the blockchain secure. Seed nodes can't produce blocks!

How to vote in the CLI

Download the 2.0.0 version of the X-Cash binaries, on our official website or on the GitHub Releases.

You will need to synchronize your wallet to a node, or download your own blockchain locally.

Once the wallet is loaded, type the following command: vote DELEGATES_PUBLIC_ADDRESS or DELEGATES_NAME

You can either put the DELEGATES_PUBLIC_ADDRESS which is a standard XCASH public address, or the DELEGATES_NAME.

You will get a success message when your vote has been taken into account.


For more details, see the detailed documentation: Vote & Staking -> Desktop Wallet .

Changing your vote in the CLI

You can change your vote by voting for a new delegate, and the old vote will automatically be replaced with the new vote: vote DELEGATES_PUBLIC_ADDRESS or DELEGATES_NAME

The wallet will create a reserve proof with the entirety of the wallet and assign it to the designated delegate. Once your vote has been casted, you will have to wait with your wallet running until the top of hour before getting a success message.

You will get a success message when your vote has been taken into account.

 How to vote in the android wallet

In case you have already generated a wallet, voting from the Android X-Cash Wallet APP is a very simple process. (Please be aware that the Android wallet is still in Beta)

  1. Open the X-Cash APP
  2. Tap to resume and type your wallet password.
  3. Wait to synchronize with the network.
  4. Once synced, tap the DPOPS button in the upper right corner
  5. Scroll down the list to find your desired delegate and tap it’s name to select it, or type it’s name in the “Delegate Name” textbox near the “Vote” button
  6. With the desired delegate’s name in the text box, tap the Vote button
  7. In the next window, you will have two choices:
    • If you are in the 3rd minute of the hour, you can tap Quick Vote and the vote will be placed right away
    • If you are out of the 3rd minute, you can tap Auto Waiting and the app will place the vote after the needed waiting time (you need to maintain the wallet opened till you will receive the confirmation).

For more details, see the detailed documentation: Vote & Staking -> Android Wallet .

How to register a delegate

Once the wallet is loaded, type the following command: delegate_register DELEGATE_NAME IP_ADDRESS|DOMAIN_NAME BLOCK_VERIFIER_PUBLIC_KEY

And replace the information with:

  • DELEGATE_NAME
    the name that will be displayed on the delegate explorer. Cannot be updated.
  • IP_ADDRESS|DOMAIN_NAME
    your server's IP address or its domain name (if you have bought a domain name and correctly set up the DNS record). It is possible to change this information at a later time. Can be updated.
  • BLOCK_VERIFIER_PUBLIC_KEY
    the block verifier public key that you have generated earlier. Cannot be updated.

For more details, see the detailed documentation: Register Delegate -> Register Yourself as a Delegate .

How to update a delegates data

Once the wallet is loaded, type the following command: delegate_update ITEM (about, website, team, shared_delegate_status, delegate_fee and server_specs)

  • about - Short description about you and your motivations as a delegate.
  • website - Link to your landing page or website relating to your delegate information and statistics.
  • team - In case of delegate node managed by several persons. Can be a team name, names or GitHub profiles.
  • shared_delegate_status - shared | group | solo
  • delegate_fee - Fees (in percentage) taken by the shared delegate on the block reward.
  • server_specs - A description of the servers' specifications and hardware.

Examples:

delegate_update website my-delegate-website.com
delegate_update team Manager: @tic | Treasury: @tac
delegate_update delegate_fee 12.354321

For more details, see the detailed documentation: Register Delegate -> Update Public Information .
